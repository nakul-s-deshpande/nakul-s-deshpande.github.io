# Research
I am a scientist working at the intersection of geophysics, geomorphology, and soft-matter/granular physics. 

Earth’s surface is a granular-fluid interface, home to rolling hills, badlands, Arctic tundra, coastlines, deep-sea trenches, dune fields, steep mountains, glacial valleys, meandering rivers, and the ground beneath our feet. Grains of sediment on these landscapes are nudged about by wind and water, and relentlessly pulled by gravity. These are simple ingredients. Yet they give rise to [endless forms most beautiful](https://www.wikiwand.com/en/Endless_Forms_Most_Beautiful_(book). 

My motivation for science is rooted in the freedom to think and work creatively on the dynamics of landscapes at scales from the granular to the tectonic.

Interested in hearing more? Check out this [spotlight](https://connect.agu.org/epsp/spotlight/june-2020).
